# Key Concepts Learned

## Reconnaissance

The attacker gathers information about the target using passive and active techniques. This includes identifying domains, employees, technologies, exposed services, and potential attack vectors.

---

## Weaponization

After reconnaissance, the attacker prepares the required exploit, malware, phishing document, or malicious payload that will be used during the attack.

---

## Delivery

The prepared payload is delivered to the victim through methods such as phishing emails, malicious websites, USB devices, or compromised software.

---

## Exploitation

The attacker exploits a vulnerability or user interaction to execute malicious code or gain unauthorized access to the target environment.

---

## Installation

Once access is obtained, persistence mechanisms such as malware, scheduled tasks, or backdoors are installed to maintain long-term access.

---

## Command and Control (C2)

The compromised system establishes communication with an attacker-controlled server to receive commands, transfer stolen information, or download additional payloads.

---

## Actions on Objectives

The attacker performs the intended goal, such as stealing sensitive information, moving laterally, escalating privileges, deploying ransomware, or disrupting business operations.

# Reflection

Completing this room strengthened my understanding of how modern cyber attacks are structured. Rather than viewing attacks as isolated events, the Cyber Kill Chain demonstrates that successful intrusions follow a sequence of stages. Understanding each phase helps security professionals detect malicious activity earlier, implement defensive controls, and interrupt attacks before attackers achieve their objectives.

# Key Findings

The practical exercises demonstrated that many real-world vulnerabilities originate from improper authorization and flawed business logic rather than complex software exploits.

The analyzed reports reinforced several important security principles:

- Authentication alone is insufficient.
- Authorization must be validated on every request.
- Business logic vulnerabilities can significantly impact organizations.
- Threat modeling improves defensive planning.
- Understanding attacker methodology improves defensive capabilities.

---

# Challenges Encountered

During the task several challenges were encountered:

- Understanding Cyber Kill Chain applicability for web vulnerabilities.
- Mapping web application vulnerabilities to MITRE ATT&CK techniques.
- Learning Maltego relationship visualization.
- Selecting appropriate ATT&CK techniques for business logic vulnerabilities.

These challenges were resolved through additional research, practical experimentation, and iterative analysis.

---

# Lessons Learned

The most important lessons learned include:

- Think like an attacker before thinking like a defender.
- Every user-controlled parameter should be considered untrusted.
- Server-side authorization must always be enforced.
- Business context is essential during security assessments.
- Documentation is as important as technical analysis.
